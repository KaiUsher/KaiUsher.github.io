Project summary
The what and the why.

PQ is a platform designed to promote well-being and an active lifestyle within corporate organisations. Colleagues create teams and record physical activites which are converted into PQ Points. These points are tracked and celebrated across various leaderboards, with the top individuals and teams being invited to a physical corporate experience.

With a focus on inclusivity, PQ allows everyone in an organisation to compete on a level playing field; no matter your fitness level, PQ is designed to be engaging and rewarding.

The web-application is a technically involved platform, with multiple systems working in harmony allowing users to connect their preferred tracking devices (Garmin, Strava, etc.) and provide a seamless experience across desktop and mobile.

Initial research
Discovering the needs of the user.

In order to gauge the interest and discover the potential market for the platform a number of discussions took place with organisations of varying sizes.

Whilst the basis of the conversation was structured around a questionnaire, it was vital the discussion was kept open and natural to ensure the true needs of the companies could be discovered. This was achieved by using techniques such as 'The 5 Whys' to help determine what the real problems related to employee wellbeing were/are.

The insights of these conversations were then organised into categories such as 'problems', 'existing solutions' and 'success metrics', which would come together to help us define what a successful project would consist of.

Examples of some of the planning documentation that was created at the start of the project.

Planning
Defining the requirements and user stories.

With the information we gathered from the user interviews a list of requirements was compiled. These requirements were then filtered down and prioritised using the MoSCoW method.

Once the requirements were scoped out, they were then translated into user stories to ensure the end user was always at the focus of development sprints.

"As an employee, I want my activities to sync automatically so they can contribute to my totals."

"As an employee, I want to be able to ‘like’ my colleagues’ activities so I can show support."

"As an admin, I want to be able to invite employees via their company emails so they can sign up to the game."

"As an admin, I want to be able to view an analytics report so I can understand how my employees are engaging with the game."

Wireframing
Initial designs and prototyping.

Wireframes were created in Adobe XD and turned into prototypes for testing user journeys and high-level page structure.

An example wireframe of the main activity feed.

Mock-ups
Higher-fidelity designs of the platform.

With the basic layout and structure confirmed from the wireframe testing, the designs were then refined and high-fidelity mock-ups were created.

These mock-ups were essentially fully-functioning prototypes that allowed the wider team to test user flows, ensure information hierarchy was correct and finalise which features were on the critical path (and which could be held back for later development sprints).

An early feed mock-up testing the addition of interactive elements on the feed. In this case, the 'wellbeing check-in' element was being explored to see how the platform could both push materials to employees who needed it the most and determine how activity level impacted employee stress.

A mock-up of the Leaderboards section of the platform.

A mock-up of the Challenges section of the platform.

As part of the design process, responsive implementations were considered from the very beginning. Due to the fact the PQ platform would solely exist as a web application, it was vital that the mobile experience was just as seamless as the desktop one.

Some of the mobile mock-ups for the key pages of the platform.

Building
Continuous delivery and testing.

Following an Agile methodology, the platform was built using the user stories as a basis for sprints. Weekly releases meant that progress was always visible and changes were being testing right from the beginning of the development period.

As and when problems were discovered or potential improvements were thought of, the designs were revisited to account for them.

The end result of this process was a platform that was highly tailored to the needs of the end user whilst retaining the ability to scale in the future.

Two pages of the final platform on desktop and mobile.

Going forward
The future of PQ.

At the time of writing, the initial delivery of the project to end-users/companies has just happened and the project is now being used commercially.

The plans for the future are therefore focused on gathering feedback, usage stats and having conversations with the users of PQ. With this information we can cycle back to the start of the design process and informatively move forward with new features and improvements.
